Health Reimbusement Account (HRA)

Benefits may vary or be unavailable in some locations or eligibility groups.


What is a Health Reimbursement Account?

A Health Reimbursement Account, or HRA, is a special account funded by JBS to pay for the first $500 (employee only) or $1,000 (employee + family*) of eligible medical expenses. This account is only available when you enroll in the CDHP medical plan.


How do I use it?

Your first eligible medical expenses such as deductibles and coinsurance will be automatically paid out of this account until you use up all your funds.


What happens if I don’t spend all the money in my HRA?

Any money you don’t spend by the end of the year will remain in the account and roll over to the next year (as long as you are employed by JBS and enrolled in the CDHP). This allows you to build up a savings for future medical expenses, and it’s possible you may not spend any of your own money on health care services!


What happens if I use all of my HRA funds before the end of the year?

If you spend all of your HRA money before the end of the year, you will be responsible for all healthcare costs until you meet your deductible and out-of-pocket maximum. If you need a lot of medical care and expect to use all of your HRA funds, you can contribute to a Health Care Flexible Spending Account to cover the remaining expenses.

*Includes Employee + Spouse, Employee + Children, and Employee + Family rate tiers

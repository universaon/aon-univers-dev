Life Insurance

Benefits may vary or be unavailable in some locations or eligibility groups.


Basic Term Life and Accidental Death & Dismemberment Insurance

The Company provides basic term life and accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D) coverage at no cost to you and enrollment is automatic once you become eligible.

Basic Term Life pays a benefit to your beneficiaries in the event of your death. Accidental Death and Dismemberment is paid, in addition to the life insurance benefit, if you die in a covered accident. It also pays if you suffer a covered dismemberment, such as loss of vision or a limb.

Supplemental Life and Accidental Death & Dismemberment Insurance

Supplemental Life and AD&D benefits provide additional coverage on top of the company-paid Life and AD&D benefits. Benefit offerings vary by location and eligibility group. See your 2017 Benefits Summary or speak with a benefits counselor for details.


Whole Life Insurance

Unum’s Whole Life Insurance is designed to pay a death benefit to your beneficiaries but it can also gain cash value you can use while you are living. Whole Life Insurance can accumulate cash value (guaranteed minimum rate of 4.5%). ** Over time you can borrow from the cash value or use it to buy a reduced policy with no more premiums due.

You may also purchase a Whole Life policy for your spouse, children, stepchildren, legally adopted children and grandchildren, age 14 days until their 26th birthday. It is an affordable, easy way to start an individual policy for them.

Additional Coverage Options

Living Benefit Option Rider
You can request up to 100% of the death benefit amount (to a maximum of $60,000) if you are diagnosed with a medical condition that limits life expectancy to 12 months (24 months in IL, MA or WA) or less. Any payout you receive while you are living would reduce the amount of benefit that you would be paid to your beneficiaries when you die.

Children Term Life Benefit
With the purchase of an employee policy, this benefit covers all eligible children. Coverage is available at one affordable premium. The policy ends when your policy ends or when the last child turns 25. At that time, children are guaranteed the right to buy an individual Whole Life policy at five times the amount of their rider. Coverage will be cancelled if employee coverage is cancelled.

(In WA, the Child Term Benefit is not available.)

How much life insurance do you need?


Many financial experts recommend you have at least five to eight times your household income in life insurance. To calculate the level sufficient to cover your needs, you should consider your current income and how much it costs to maintain your family's standard of living. You should also consider your current expenses and your family's future financial needs such as the following:
    Current Expenses
  • Home Mortgage
  • Car Payments
  • Credit Card Debt
  • Other Debt
    Future Needs
  • Child Care
  • College Tuition
  • Spouse's Retirement
  • Routine Household Expenses

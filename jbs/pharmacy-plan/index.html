Pharmacy Plan

CVS Caremark

Benefits may vary or be unavailable in some locations or eligibility groups.


The pharmacy plan is included with your medical plan at no additional cost. Prescription drugs are covered in-network only with different copays for each tier. You can choose to fill your prescriptions at a retail pharmacy or through the mail order service.

The plan has four tiers that determine how much you pay out of pocket for prescriptions.

Tier 1: Generic
Tier 2: Preferred Brand – brand medications on the formulary list
Tier 3: Non-Preferred Brand – brand name medications not on the formulary list
Tier 4: Specialty

See your 2017 Benefits Summary for copays for each tier.

The formulary list is available from your local HR representative. The formulary is a list of prescription drugs that are “preferred” by the plan. These drugs are determined by the insurance company to be the safest, most effective, and least expensive medications to treat certain conditions. You will pay more for medications that are not included on the formulary list.

NOTE: If you choose to purchase preferred or non-preferred brand name drugs when a generic equivalent is available, you will be required to pay the difference between the cost of the generic and preferred/non-preferred brand name drug, plus the preferred brand name copay.

There are no out-of-network prescription benefits. Only generic oral contraceptives are covered. HRA funds cannot be used to cover prescription copays.

2017 Pharmacy Benefits

The Pharmacy Benefits Manager is CVS Caremark. The formulary list is available from your HR representative. Target pharmacies are now CVS pharmacies. Walgreens is out of the network.

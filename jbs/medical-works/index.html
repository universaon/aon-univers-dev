The CDHP Medical Plan

The CDHP medical plan offers great value regardless of your health care needs. Preventive care – like annual physicals and screenings – is always covered 100% in-network. For all other services, the plan includes a Company-funded Health Reimbursement Account that you can use toward eligible expenses. Plus, any money you don’t spend can be saved for future medical expenses.

If you use the plan wisely, it’s possible that you won’t spend any of your own money on healthcare.

Your healthcare needs: How the plan covers them:
Annual physicals, immunizations, health screening tests The plan pays 100% of the costs.
Office visits, specialists, urgent care, emergency room visits, hospital stays You pay 100% of plan allowance until you reach the deductible. Your Health Reimbursement Account covers your first eligible expenses.
Healthcare services after you meet the deductible You pay 20% and the plan pays 80% of the in-network costs until you reach the out-of-pocket maximum.
Healthcare services after you reach the out-of-pocket maximum The plan pays 100% of the costs for the remainder of the year.

Medical Plan FAQs

Q: What is Consumer Driven Health Plan (CDHP)?

A CDHP is a type of health insurance that allows you, as a participant, to use a Health Reimbursement Account (HRA), to pay health care expenses directly.

Q: What is a Health Reimbursement Account (HRA)?

The HRA is a plan sponsored by JBS and Pilgrim's Pride that can be used to reimburse a portion of you and your eligible family members' out-of-pocket medical expenses, such as deductibles and coinsurance. It is not an insurance program but a financial reimbursement plan funded entirely by JBS and Pilgrim’s Pride. Unused account balances will be rolled over to the following plan year, provided JBS and Pilgrim’s Pride continue to offer the program and you remain enrolled in it.

Q: What is so different about the CDHP with HRA?

Use of the HRA makes the CDHP truly different from health plans you may have had in the past. The HRA is similar to a savings account designated specifically for eligible health-related expenses, such as deductible, copays, and/or medical expenses that are not covered by your health plan.

Q: What are the tax advantages of an HRA?

Reimbursements made from JBS and Pilgrim’s Pride through the HRA are not considered part of your income and are not taxed.

Q: How much do JBS and Pilgrim’s Pride contribute to the HRA?

$500 for employee only, $1,000 for family (employee plus spouse, child(ren) or family).

Q: Who do I call for questions about the CDHP?

Call the JBS/Pilgrim’s Pride Benefits Help Line at 1-855-327-5911.

Q: What medical expenses can be reimbursed from the HRA?

Qualified medical expenses include those expenses defined by the CDHP. These expenses may include deductibles and coinsurance.

Q: What expenses are typically not reimbursable?

The following expenses are typically not reimbursable:

  • Medical expenses that are not defined as eligible expenses by the CDHP
  • Medical expenses that do not meet IRS section 213(d) requirements
  • Medical expenses incurred by you or your spouse or eligible dependents before your participation in the CDHP was effective

Q: If I am enrolled in both the HRA and the Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA), which account will be used first?

The HRA and the Health Care FSA, while separate accounts, provide reimbursement of qualified medical expenses as defined by the medical plan and the IRS (i.e., deductibles and coinsurance). Should you have both accounts, expenses eligible under both plans will be reimbursed through the HRA first, then default to the Health Care FSA.

Q: What happens to the HRA money if I am no longer enrolled in the CDHP?

The account reverts back to JBS and Pilgrim’s Pride if you change plans or leave the company (unless COBRA is elected).

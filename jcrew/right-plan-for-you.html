WHAT IS THE RIGHT PLAN FOR YOU?

MEET GARY

Gary is a young, single non-tobacco user. He typically gets sick enough to see his physician only once a year, besides his annual exam.  The full cost of his care is $350.

If covered under the PPO, Gary has a copay of $40 for a specialist visit.  He pays the full rate under the CCP until he reaches his deductible.

  PPO CCP
Medical Costs $40 $350
HSA Contribution $0 $400
Gary’s Out-of-Pocket $60 $0*
Annual Premium $1,934 $1,381
Total Cost of Health Care $1,974 $1,381

*$50 from the J.Crew contribution is left over in his HSA for future use and cannot apply toward annual prevention.

Even though his out-ofpocket costs are higher, the CCP delivers the better Total Cost because of its lower premium and company HSA contribution.

MEET THE SMITHS

The Smiths are a family of four with a son and a daughter both active in youth sports. Each family member visits the doctor once a year (aside from their free preventive health visit). The Smith’s son fractures his leg at a soccer game, which adds some unexpected medical expense. None of the Smiths use tobacco.

The full cost of services is $3600. Under the CCP, the Smiths reach their $3,000 deductible, after which the plan picks up 80 percent of costs.

  PPO CCP
Medical Costs $670 $3,032
HSA Contribution $0 $800
Gary’s Out-of-Pocket $670 $2,232
Annual Premium $6,188 $4,420
Total Cost of Health Care $6,858 $6,652

The PPO has the lower out-ofpocket costs by far. However, the premium savings and the HSA contribution make the CCP the lower cost health plan overall.

These samples are for illustrative purposes only and are intended to show how the two plan options work differently. The acutal medical cost you experience and the final total cost of the plans will vary.

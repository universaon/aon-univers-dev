HBC WELLNESS INCENTIVE

Living a healthy lifestyle is key to improving and maintaining good health. HBC is taking steps to encourage you to make healthy living a priority.

The primary initiative to promote good health, is the Wellness Incentive. You can earn incentive dollars in your HSA for 2016 when you complete your Health Assessment and free biometric screening.

What is the Wellness Incentive?

  • If you are enrolled in Associate Only coverage under the Gold or Silver medical plan options, and you complete the wellness requirements, you are eligible to receive a $250* HBC employer contribution into your HSA.
  • If you are enrolled in Associate + Spouse/Domestic Partner or Family coverage under the Gold or Silver medical plan options, and you AND your covered spouse/domestic partner complete the wellness requirements, you are eligible to receive a $500* employer contribution into your HSA.
  • If you are enrolled in Associate + Child(ren) coverage under the Gold or Silver medical plan options, only you have to complete the wellness requirements to earn up to the full $500* wellness incentive. Children are not eligible to complete the free biometric screening.

*The Wellness Incentive contributions are in addition to the automatic employer contributions you receive for enrolling in the Gold or Silver HBC medical plan and opening an HSA.

What are the wellness requirements?

To be eligible for the Wellness Incentive, you must complete the following requirements:

  • A Cigna Health Assessment, AND
  • A biometric screening

IMPORTANT: If you cover a spouse or domestic partner under an HBC medical plan, you BOTH must complete the requirements to be eligible for the Wellness Incentive.

The Wellness Incentive HSA contributions are pro-rated based on the completion date of your wellness requirements. Deposits will be made as soon as administratively possible. Next Steps

Take the Health Assessment

The online health assessment is a brief, confidential questionnaire that evaluates your current health and indicates if you are at risk for certain conditions. It also provides recommendations on steps towards better health.

Follow these steps to complete your Health Assessment:

  1. Register and/or log into your MyCigna account at www.myCigna.com
  2. Click on “Take My Health Assessment”
  3. Complete the Assessment and view your results

Cigna will automatically report your completion to HBC for purposes of the Wellness Incentive. Your personal information will NOT be shared with HBC.

Complete a Biometric Screening

A biometric screening measures important health metrics, such as your blood pressure, Body Mass Index (BMI), and cholesterol levels. These metrics, or numbers, provide insight into your well-being and identify potential health problems. They are a starting point on the path to better health. Depending on your location, you may have three possible options to complete your Biometric Screening.

1. Free On-Site Biometric Screenings (for Associates ONLY)

There will be free on-site biometric screenings at select HBC locations. These screenings will begin mid-February and end on March 31, 2016. Dates by location will be included in the scheduling system.

To make an appointment for an on-site screening, follow the steps below:

  1. Go to My.QuestForHealth.com to Register
  2. Enter Registration Key: HBC2016
  3. Enter your Full Legal Name and Date of Birth as it appears in HBC’s system, including Jr or III, if applicable
  4. Select the event you want to attend and choose a time that works best for you.

If your location’s event isn’t visible, you are not yet able to schedule an appointment, as registration has not yet begun. You can still schedule an appointment for a PSC or use the downloadable Physician Form available on the scheduler at My.QuestForHealth.com.

Registration will begin on February 1st for all Patient Service Center appointments and screenings, as well as for the Physician Form option. There are even same day appointments available in many areas. Registration for the events will begin as they become available in your area. Please keep in mind, the scheduler will close for registration two weeks prior to the event date. Be sure to register early so you can get an appointment before it closes.

If you want to call in and get registered through the call center support center, or have any questions about the scheduling process, please feel free to contact: Blueprint For Wellness Center at 1- 855-623-9355 (Monday – Friday 7:00 am to 8:30 pm, CST and Saturday 7:30 am – 4 pm CST).

Once your screening is completed, your results will automatically be uploaded to your online account, and Cigna will be notified of your completion for purposes of the Wellness Incentive.

2. Free Off-Site Biometric Screening

If you are unable to participate in an on-site biometric screening, you and your spouse/domestic partner (if applicable) can complete your screening at a Quest Diagnostics Patient Service Center (PSC) or at your physician’s office during your annual well visit anytime between 1/1/2016-12/31/2016.

To make an appointment for an off-site screening, follow the steps below:

  1. Go to My.QuestForHealth.com to Register
  2. Select ‘PSC’
  3. To select a nearby Quest PSC, use the zip code locator and find the closest Quest PSC to you as well as their contact information and address.
  4. Schedule an appointment (some are even same day), or register as a walk-in and go at your convenience.

3. The Physician Form

To complete your Biometric Screening using the Physician Form option, follow the steps below:

  1. Access the form by registering at My.QuestForHealth.com and selecting ‘Physician’s Form’
  2. Print the form
  3. Schedule an appointment** with your doctor ( the results must be received no later than 12/31 by Quest Diagnostics and are for appointments 1/1/2016-12/31/2016)
  4. Have your physician complete the form and submit it by fax to Quest Diagnostics at the number provided on the form

** When scheduling an appointment, it is important that you mention it is for your annual well visit. Annual well visits are considered preventive, and the cost of an in-network visit is covered in full by your medical plan.

Why Assessments and Screenings are Important

Anyone can be on the path to a serious health condition without ever realizing it before it’s too late. The Health Assessment and biometric screening are intended to get a snapshot of your current health. This makes it possible for you and your physician to identify potential conditions such as heart disease or diabetes, and set up a plan to keep you healthy.

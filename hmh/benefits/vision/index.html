Vision

Vision Coverage Issue

If you are a benefit-eligible team member and you selected vision coverage for the 2017 plan year, you may have received a letter from UnitedHealthcare Vision advising you that your vision coverage in 2016 was terminated. This letter was sent out in error by UnitedHealthcare Vision.

Although UnitedHealthcare Vision does not send out membership ID cards, please be assured that you have vision plan coverage for the 2017 plan year. If you would like to verify your coverage, visit www.myuhcvision.com. You will also be able to print out an ID card from the web site. More details about the vision plan are available at Flexible Benefits, TeamMeridian.com.

The Vision Plan is designed to help pay the cost of eye exams, eyeglasses, and medically necessary contact lenses for you and your family, up to age 26, once every 12 months. Vision benefits are administered by UnitedHealthcare.


UnitedHealthcare Vision Plan

If you receive covered services through UnitedHealthcare’s network of providers, the plan will generally pay 100% after you pay a $10 or $20 copayment (depending on the service). If you go out-side the network, the plan pays less and you pay more.

The UnitedHealthcare Vision Plan pays benefits based on a large selection of prescription frames, lenses, and contact lenses. If you choose a type of frame or non-standard cosmetic feature that is not part of the plan’s regular selection, you will pay more. For contact lenses outside of the regular selection, the plan will pay up to a maximum of $150 per year when you use a network provider, or up to a maximum of $105 when you use an out-of-network provider. The plan covers up to six boxes of disposable contact lenses when chosen from the covered contact lens selection.


To find a provider near you, call UnitedHealthcare at (800) 638-3120, or visit www.myuhcvision.com.

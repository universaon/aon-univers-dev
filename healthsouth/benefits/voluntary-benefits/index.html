Voluntary Benefits

Group Hospital Indemnity Insurance

A trip to the hospital can be stressful…and so can the bills.

Health insurance isn’t always enough to cover all the expenses associated with a hospital stay. Hospital Indemnity Insurance works as a supplement to your medical coverage, paying benefits that can help offset out-of-pocket expenses.

The plan pays a lump sum benefit directly to you if you or a covered family member is admitted to the hospital for a covered accident or sickness. You can use this benefit any way you choose to cover expenses the medical plan may not cover, such as coinsurance and deductibles.


    What is covered?
  • Hospital Admission
  • Daily Hospital Confinement
  • Emergency Room Treatment
  • Intensive Care Confinement
  • Ambulance Transport
    Plan Features:
  • Coverage is guaranteed issue if you enroll when you are first eligible.
  • Coverage is available for your spouse and children.
  • Premiums are paid through convenient payroll deductions.

Rates

Rates will be provided at the time of your enrollment.

The policy or its provisions may vary or be unavailable in some states. The policy has exclusions and limitations that may affect any benefits payable.

Group Critical Illness Insurance

The out-of-pocket costs of a serious illness can be catastrophic, even with medical insurance.

Critical Illness Insurance helps provide financial protection in the event of a covered serious illness. The policy pays a lump sum benefit directly to you if you are diagnosed with a covered condition. You can use this benefit any way you choose — to cover deductibles and coinsurance, to pay expenses your family incurs to be by your side, or simply to replace lost earnings from being out of work. You choose the benefit amount when you enroll.


Covered illnesses include: Additional covered illnesses for dependent children include:
  • Blindness
  • Coma (as the result of severe traumatic brain injury)
  • Coronary Artery Bypass Surgery*
  • End-Stage Renal (Kidney) Failure
  • Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
  • Major Organ Failure
  • Occupational HIV
  • Permanent Paralysis (as the result of a covered accident)
  • Stroke
  • Cancer
  • Carcinoma in situ*
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Cleft Lip or Palate
  • Cystic Fibrosis
  • Down Syndrome
  • Spina Bifida

    Plan Features:
  • You do not have to be terminally ill to receive benefits.
  • Family coverage is available.
  • A Wellness Benefit is included, which pays an annual benefit if a covered health screening test is performed (blood test, stress test, colonoscopies, chest X-rays, mammograms, etc.).**

Rates

Rates vary depending upon your age, tobacco-use, and the amount of coverage you elect. Rates will be provided at the time of your enrollment.

*The coverage pays 25% of the face amount of the policy once per lifetime for coronary artery bypass surgery and carcinoma in situ.
**Wellness Benefit may not be available in all states.
The policy or its provisions may vary or be unavailable in some states. The policy has exclusions and limitations that may affect any benefits payable.

Group Accident Insurance

Accidents happen. You can’t always prevent them, but you can take steps to reduce the financial impact.

Accident Insurance is designed to help cover the out-of-pocket medical expenses and extra bills that can follow a covered accident. The policy pays a benefit directly to you – not a doctor or hospital – and you can use the money however you choose. Benefit amounts are based on the type of injury and treatment needed.


Covered injuries and accident-related expenses include: Plan Features:
  • Hospitalization
  • Physical therapy
  • Transportation
  • Lodging for family
  • Hospital intensive care
  • Benefits are paid for accidents that occur off-the-job
  • You can also cover your spouse and children.
  • There are no health questions or physical exams required

Rates

Rates will be provided at the time of your enrollment

The policy or its provisions may vary or be unavailable in some states. The policy has exclusions and limitations that may affect any benefits payable.


Critical Illness and Accident Insurance — Complements to Your Health Plan

Critical Illness and Accident Insurance are excellent complements to your medical plan. Both plans pay benefits that can be used to offset out-of-pocket medical expenses, like deductibles, copays and prescription drugs that arise when a severe illness or injury strikes.

Whole (Permanent) Life Insurance

Can your family maintain their lifestyle without you? Whole Life Insurance helps ensure they can.

Whole (Permanent) Life Insurance is designed to pay a death benefit to your beneficiaries if you pass away. Unlike term life insurance, permanent life insurance doesn’t have an expiration date (or term). Since the coverage doesn’t expire, your premiums will stay the same for as long as you hold the policy.


  • You can purchase coverage for yourself, your spouse, your children, and/or your grandchildren.
  • Whole Life Insurance is voluntary, which means you can purchase the precise amount of coverage that is right for your needs.
  • The benefit is guaranteed issue when first offered, so no physical exams are required to apply for coverage up to a certain amount.
  • Coverage is portable – you can take your policy with you if you change jobs or retire.

Rates

Rates vary depending upon your age, the amount of coverage you elect, and other such factors. Rates will be provided at the time of your enrollment.

The policy or its provisions may vary or be unavailable in some states. The policy has exclusions and limitations which may affect any benefits payable.

How much life insurance do you need?

To calculate the level sufficient to cover your needs, you should consider your current income and how much it costs to maintain your family’s standard of living. You should also consider your current expenses and your family’s future financial needs, such as the following:

    Current Expenses:
  • Mortgage or Rent
  • Car Payments
  • Credit Card Debt
  • Other Debt
    Future Needs:
  • Child Care
  • College Tuition
  • Spouse’s Retirement
  • Routine Household Expenses

About Your Options

Your medical options are designed to provide you and your family with access to quality, affordable health care.

All of your medical options cover a broad range of health care services and supplies. They differ in how they share costs with you and how they provide access to care.

You have two plans to choose from – the QualCare HMO and the QualCare PPO.

The QualCare PPO offers expanded in-network options along with an out-of-network option.


How Do the Medical Plans Work?

(If you are on a mobile device-please scroll to view the full contents)

QualCare HMO QualCare PPO
The QualCare HMO is an Open Access Plan that allows you to receive care through the Inner Circle, as well as the QualCare provider network. This plan covers preventive care, outpatient care and hospitalization services delivered by participating physicians, specialists and facilities. Since this HMO is an Open Access Plan, you do not need to select a Primary Care Physician (PCP) to coordinate your care or obtain a referral from a participating PCP or specialist in order to access care. The QualCare PPO Plan allows you and each covered family member to obtain services both in- and out-of-network. The in-network approach allows you to seek care without having to designate a Primary Care Physician (PCP). As a result, it is not necessary to obtain a referral before visiting another physician or specialist. The out-of-network approach provides the participant with the ability to see any physician, but at a greater cost. This plan offers lower deductibles and higher reimbursement levels when Inner Circle and/or in-network providers are utilized.

NOTE: All providers must be participating in order to receive the in-network benefit.

Choosing the Medical Plan That’s Right For You

There is no right or wrong answer when choosing a medical plan. However, before deciding, you should consider the following:

  • Whether or not the health care providers and facilities you routinely visit belong to the QualCare network
  • The level of network access you would like to have available (Inner Circle, In-Network, Out-of-Network)
  • The type of provider your frequently visit (Inner Circle, In-Network, Out-of-Network)
  • How often you use your medical benefits and for what services
  • The cost per pay period for each medical plan option
  • The amount you would be required to pay out-of-pocket (copays, deductibles and coinsurance) for medical services you typically require (e.g. doctor’s office visits, diagnostic tests, preventive care, etc.


Once you have taken the time to evaluate your options, you will be able to make an informed decision about what plan will best meet your personal health care needs.

In order to receive the Inner Circle benefit for a hospital admission, you may use either Perth Amboy or Old Bridge. If the services cannot be performed at RBMC, you may use any Hackensack Meridian Health hospital only to receive the 90% reimbursement. If you elect to use another QualCare hospital it will be paid at the In-Network (75%) rate or if you use a hospital not in QualCare’s network, you will be covered at the Out-of-Network (60%) rate.


IN-NETWORK BENEFITS

When you obtain “in-network” services, QualCare pays for eligible expenses when these services are obtained from a provider within its network. QualCare reimburses for both your Primary Care Physician and participating Specialists at the allowances identified in your plan summaries on a fee-for-service basis. You receive the highest level of benefits when using in-network providers, and you do not have to file claims (however, you are responsible for all copays, deductibles and coinsurance).


OUT-OF-NETWORK BENEFITS

When you obtain out-of-network services, QualCare pays for eligible services not obtained from one of the providers in the network. However, you will be responsible for paying a greater share of the cost when you obtain services out-of-network. Non-network providers are reimbursed up to the applicable allowance (see plan summaries). An annual deductible and coinsurance apply to all eligible medical and most supplemental services. Once you reach the out-of-pocket maximum, the plan pays 100% of the appropriate allowance for eligible services for the rest of the year.

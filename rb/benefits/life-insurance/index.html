Life Insurance


Group Life and Accidental Death & Dismemberment Insurance

Raritan Bay Medical Center provides all full-time and part-time employees who work 20 or more hours per week with Group Life Insurance and Accidental Death and Dismemberment (AD&D) Insurance in an amount that equals one-and-a-half times their annual salary to a maximum of $150,000 at no cost. This benefit is provided through Unum.

Supplemental Life Insurance


Eligible employees are also given the option to purchase additional (supplemental) life coverage for themselves, their spouse and child(ren) through Unum up to prescribed limits. The coverage specifics below outline purchase amounts and maximums for Basic Life and AD&D Insurance as well as Supplemental Life Insurance.

Life Insurance/AD&D Coverage Specifics


  • The Group Life and AD&D maximum benefit is $150,000.

  • Eligible employees can purchase Supplemental Life Insurance in an amount equal to 1x, 2x or 3x their base salary to a maximum of $500,000.

  • You can purchase Supplement Life Insurance for your dependents in the following amounts:
      
    • Spouse - can be purchased in a flat amount equal to 50% of the coverage amount elected by the employee to a maximum of $250,000.

    • Child(ren) - $100 flat amount for children age 14 days to six months and in increments of $5,000 up to a maximum benefit of $10,000 per child age six months to 19 years. Benefit can be extended to age 25 if dependent is a full-time student.

  • You must purchase Supplemental Life Insurance for yourself in order to elect coverage for your spouse and/or dependent child(ren).

  • The Guaranteed Issue amount for Supplemental Life Insurance (does not require Evidence of Insurability when initially eligible) is $100,000 for employee, $50,000 for spouse and $10,000 for child(ren).

  • Payroll deduction amounts for Supplement Life Insurance are based on the age of the employee and/or spouse as of January 1st and base salary of the employee as of December 1st.

  • Life Insurance benefits reduce by 35% at age 65 for both the employee and spouse.

  • You must name a beneficiary – the person or persons who receive your life insurance amount. If you do not name a beneficiary, the benefit will be paid to your estate.

EVIDENCE OF INSURABILITY (EOI)


Evidence of Insurability is required for all Supplemental Life Insurance purchase amounts above $100,000 (when initially eligible) for the employee and/or $50,000 for spouse. After initial eligibility EOI is required for all Supplemental Life Insurance purchase amounts.

Life insurance purchase amounts requiring EOI do not become effective (and therefore are not deducted from your pay) until approval is obtained from the insurer. Click here to access the Life Insurance EOI form.

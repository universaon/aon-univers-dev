Meet Gary.

Gary is a young, single adult. He lives a healthy lifestyle. He typically gets sick enough to see his physician once per year besides his free wellness exam.

  • Default
  • Standard Care
  • Specialist Care
  • Broken Arm
  • Heart Attack

Click buttons to see how each event affects your total cost.

Using the HSA Plan, not only does Gary save money from the start through lower premiums, but he spends nothing out of pocket, as his medical costs are covered by the contribution into his HSA. Even better, he saved $170 that will stay in his HSA for future health needs.

Gary has a condition that requires a couple of visits to a specialist, along with some diagnostic tests. The total cost of these services is $700, but Gary still comes out ahead.

A bike accident resulted in a broken arm. Physician fees, x-rays, a cast and other items cost $1,400 in the end. With this unpleasant surprise, the total cost of health care between the two plans is nearly even.

With Accident Insurance Gary received a $3,500 lump sum payment to fix his broken arm and payed nothing out-of-pocket.

Gary had a heart attack and the costs associated with his recovery were about $9000 on top of his routine expenses.

Fortunately, Gary purchased $5,000 in critical illness coverage for $4 per month (premium rates depend on age and tobacco status). The critical illness benefit was paid to him directly when he was diagnosed. It covered all of his costs and some was left over for incidental expenses.

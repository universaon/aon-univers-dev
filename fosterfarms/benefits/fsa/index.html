Flexible Spending Account:

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) enable you to put aside money for important expenses and help you reduce your income taxes at the same time. The account allows you to set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for certain IRS approved out-of-pocket health care or dependent daycare expenses. Participant's elections are payroll deducted and placed in a special account for reimbursement to be issued as claims are incurred. Participants cannot change their individual elections unless they experience a qualifying event (i.e. marital status change, change in number of dependents, etc) as determined by their employer.

How Flexible Spending Accounts Work

  1. At your initial eligibility, you decide how much to set aside for health care and/or dependent daycare expenses.
  2. Your contributions are payroll deducted on a before-tax basis in equal installments throughout the calendar year.
  3. You have a grace period of 2 & 1/2 months after the end of the plan year in which you can use this money, which is March 15. However, all claims must be received by March 31 in order to be considered for payment.

IMPORTANT NOTE: If you elect to enroll in the Foster Farms Health Savings Plan that includes an HSA, and you currently have an FSA account, it is very important that you exhaust your FSA balance. IRS regulations do NOT allow you to open an HSA account while you have an FSA account. This means Foster Farms cannot fund your HSA account if you have a balance in your FSA account.

You must actively re-enroll in the FSAs each year. You are not automatically re-enrolled.

* See IRS Publications 502 and 503 for a complete list of covered expenses. Also, please note, not all groups are eligible to participate in this program.

Considering the Health Savings Plan

I want a lower premium, giving me health savings from the start. The Health Savings Plan has a lower premium than the PPO. For example, an employee with family coverage can save between $96.57 and $117.57 per month (depending on their compensation range), which is up to $1,410 per year!

I want a chance to pay nothing out-of-pocket for health care. Foster Farms contributes $500, $1,000, or $1,500 to your HSA at the beginning of the year. This money is used to pay for out-of-pocket medical expenses. In other words, Foster Farms pays the first $500 (employee only), $1,000 (employee plus 1 dependent or employee plus children), or $1,500 (family) in medical bills.

I want to build a tax free savings account for future healthcare costs. Whatever doesn't get spent in 2016 is rolled over into your HSA for 2017. If you don't need medical services (aside from your free annual physical) in 2016, you start 2017 with $1,000, $2,000, or $3,000 in your account and that is just the employer contribution amount. It can be even higher if you contribute! It builds year after year.

Concerned About the Risk of High Deductibles?
We've Got that Covered!

You can eliminate much of the risk by combining the Health Savings Plan with voluntary benefits like Critical Illness or Accident Insurance. These voluntary benefits pay you a lump sum once you're diagnosed with a serious illness or suffer an injury. This lump sum can cover or exceed the amount of your deductible. This allows you to experience the upfront saving without the risk of being stuck paying a high deductible in case an unexpected illness or injury occurs.

Health Care Reform


How does healthcare reform affect me?

Like most people, you probably have questions about how the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act impacts you and your family. This site was created to help answer your questions and concerns.

Q: What is health care reform?

The healthcare reform law was enacted in March, 2010. The name "Affordable Care Act,” or ACA, is now used to refer to the final, amended version of the health care reform law.

The ultimate goal of the Affordable Care Act is to provide better access to affordable health care coverage for all individuals.

Q: What are marketplaces?

A marketplace is an organized exchange where you can compare and purchase private health plans. Open Enrollment in the health insurance marketplace will begin on November 1, 2016. In order for coverage to start January 1, 2017, enrollment must take place before December 15, 2016.

The last day to enroll in or change coverage through the marketplace will be January 31, 2017. You can only enroll in coverage after this date if you lose your employer based coverage or experience a qualifying life event. QLEs include birth or adoption of a child, change in marital status, or the death of a dependent.

The Affordable Care Act provides for an insurance “marketplace” in every State, structured either as a government agency or as a public/private entity. If a State has not established a marketplace, the federal Department of Health and Human Services, or HHS, operates a federally facilitated marketplace on the State's behalf. In some instances, HHS and the States jointly operate a State partnership marketplace.

Q: Will I need to pay a tax penalty?

The law requires you to have a minimum level of coverage (minimum essential coverage) or pay a tax penalty. The fee is calculated two different ways, per person or as a percentage of household income, whichever is higher.

For 2016, the per person tax is $695 per adult and $347.50 for each child under 18 years of age. There is a $2,085 limit using the per person limit. The other method is calculating 2.5 percent of household income.

For 2017, the percent will remain at 2.5 percent of household income. The per person taxes for 2017 will be available at a later date.

Health insurance plans offered in the marketplaces include a minimum set of covered items and services known as "essential benefits," and must follow established limits on cost-sharing, and certain other requirements. Health insurance issuers and your employer will provide you with a Summary of Benefits and Coverage, an easy-to-understand summary about a health plan’s benefits and coverage that will help you better understand and evaluate your health insurance choices.

Q: Am I eligible for Medicaid?

Medicaid provides health coverage for some low-income people, families and children, pregnant women, the elderly, and people with disabilities. Certain states have expanded Medicare to cover more people. You can enroll in Medicare any time of the year if you’re eligible, as there is no designated enrollment period as there is with Marketplace plans.

You can learn more about Medicaid eligibility by visiting www.HealthCare.gov. That website will also provide you with a simple link to your own State’s Medicaid website.

Q: Can I save money?

Low and moderate-income individuals and families who do not have access to affordable health insurance coverage, such as through their employer, may be eligible for federal subsidies to help them pay for the cost of coverage if elected through a marketplace.

Benefits


Medical and Prescription

Perdue offers Medical Insurance through the CareFirst BCBS BluePreferred PPO Plan. The plan includes Prescription Drug coverage through Express Scripts, at no additional cost.

Dental

The Your Perdue Benefits Dental Plans provide coverage for a wide range of dental services and supplies through Delta Dental. You may choose from one of three coverage options.

Vision

The Your Perdue Benefits Vision Plan helps you keep your eyes healthy and protected. The plan covers services at one of more than 40,000 EyeMed Vision Care network providers.

Life and AD&D Insurance

Perdue provides Basic Life Insurance at no cost to you. You may also purchase Optional Life, Dependent Life and/or Accidental Death and Dismemberment coverage as a supplement to the Company-paid benefit.

Read More Here!


Disability Insurance

Disability Insurance provides a source of income if you are ill or injured and unable to work for an extended period of time. Perdue offers Short Term Disability Insurance and Long Term Disability Insurance options.

Read More Here!


Flexible Spending Accounts

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) enable you to set aside money for out-of-pocket health care or dependent care expenses and help you reduce your income taxes at the same time.

Read More Here!


Employee Assistance Program

The GuidanceResources Employee Assistance Program (EAP) is a free service that can help you and your family members in dealing with personal, financial, and legal issues. You will have 24/7 access to confidential telephone and online support, resources, and information at no cost to you.

Read More Here!


The Perdue Savings Plan

Building a healthy financial future is just as important as taking care of your health needs today. Putting money aside for retirement is easy with the Perdue Savings Plan.

Read More Here!


Accident

Accident Insurance pays a benefit directly to you if you are injured in a covered accident. Benefit amounts are based on the type of injury you incur and treatment you need. You can use the money however you choose – to cover out-of-pocket medical expenses or other accident-related expenses.

Read More Here!


Whole Life

Whole Life Insurance pays a benefit to your beneficiaries in the event of your death. Whole Life is different than Term Life Insurance in that it is permanent coverage – it doesn’t expire as long as you pay the premiums. This means your premiums will not increase as you get older, as they do with term life.

Read More Here!


Critical Illness

Health insurance is not always enough to cover all of the expenses associated with a serious medical condition like a heart attack or cancer. Critical Illness Insurance pays a lump sum benefit directly to you, and benefits are paid in addition to any other insurance coverage you may have.

Read More Here!

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I complete my new hire enrollment?

Contact your local HR representative for instructions.

Q: Who is eligible for benefits?

Please see your enrollment packet for details.

Your eligible dependents may include:


  • Your legal opposite-sex or same-sex spouse (unless you are legally separated)
  • Your children up to age 26, regardless of marital or student status, even if they are eligible for other insurance coverage
  • Your children over age 26 who are physically or mentally disabled may be eligible (see Summary Plan Description for more details)
  • Individuals who are neither your spouse nor child(ren) whereby you have legal guardianship

Q: What are Perdue’s dependent documentation requirements?

The following documents and information are required if you wish to elect benefits for your spouse or child(ren):


  • Birth certificates are required for newborns and dependents with a different last name than yours.
  • Statements of Eligibility are required for disabled children.
  • A marriage certificate is required for a spouse with a different last name than yours.
  • Social Security numbers are required for all dependents.
  • Court orders are required for dependents who are under your guardianship.
  • Dates of birth are required for all dependents.

Q: When will my benefits take effect?

Benefit effective dates vary depending on your benefit group. Refer to your new hire benefits guide or see your local HR representative for details.

Q: Can I make changes to my elections after I’ve enrolled?

Once you’ve enrolled, you may only make changes to your coverage if you experience a qualifying life event as determined by the IRS. Examples of qualifying life events include marriage, divorce, birth or adoption of a child, change in employment status, etc. See your local HR representative for more details.

Q: Will I receive ID cards for my health insurance plans?

Yes. When you enroll in a Perdue medical, dental, or vision plan, you will receive an ID card. Cards will be mailed to your home address on file with Perdue after your enrollment is processed.

If you wish to print a temporary ID card, you can do so by visiting each plan’s website.

  • Medical: www.carefirst.com/perdue/
  • Dental: www.deltadentalins.com/
  • Vision: www.eyemedvisioncare.com

Q: How do I find a network provider within the medical plan?

Visit www.CareFirst.com/Perdue and click the Search Now button under Find a Doctor.

If you work at a location with a Perdue Wellness Center, Your Perdue Wellness Center can be your primary care provider for the Perdue Health Plan. Wellness Centers are located on-site at most of Perdue’s operations and are open to Perdue associates and dependents who are covered by the Perdue Health Plan.


Q: How do I order prescription medication by mail to save money?

Send your prescriptions to Express Scripts along with an order form and the applicable copay. Your prescriptions will be mailed to your home within two to three weeks. Visit www.express-scripts.com for details.

Q: How do I enroll in voluntary accident, critical illness or whole life insurance?

Voluntary accident, critical illness, and whole life insurance are only available during Open Enrollment held each fall. You will receive plan information and enrollment instructions closer to that time.

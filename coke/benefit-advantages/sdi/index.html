Specified Disease Insurance


Specified disease insurance (critical illness insurance) can help to protect an employee’s finances from the expenses of a serious health problem, such as a stroke, heart attack or cancer. Specified disease insurance pays a lump-sum benefit directly to the insured— not to a doctor or healthcare provider — at the first diagnosis of a covered condition.

Employees can choose a level of coverage between $10,000 and $30,000, in increments of $10,000. Coverage is also available for spouses and dependent children, and no health questions are required for this year’s enrollment.

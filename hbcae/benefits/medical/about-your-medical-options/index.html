A New Level of Choice In Health Care


HBC's medical plan options give you greater flexibility to choose a level of coverage that balances health care needs with your budget.

The plans are Consumer Driven Health Plans (CDHP) and generally carry a lower premium than traditional health plans. They also offer a Health Savings Account (HSA) that you can use to cover eligible out-of-pocket expenses.

You have three plans to choose from - Gold, Silver and Bronze.

The difference in plans is that you pay a lower premium in exchange for higher potential out-of-pocket costs. For instance, the Gold Plan carries the highest premium but has the lowest deductible and out-of-pocket maximum.

Also, if you choose the Gold or Silver plan, HBC makes a contribution into your Health Savings Account. A Health Savings Account (HSA) is a great tool for saving money for future health costs. Learn more about the Health Savings Account Here.

Things to Know

  • In-network preventive care and some preventive medications are covered at NO COST to you. Other preventative medications may be available prior to the deductible.

  • For all other services, you must meet the annual deductible before the plans pay benefits. You can use the money in your Health Savings Account to cover these expenses.

  • Once you meet your deductible, the plan pays a percentage of the cost for all other services (80% in-network under the Gold and Silver plans, 60% out-of-network, or under the Bronze plan), and you pay the rest. When you reach your annual out-of-pocket maximum, the plan pays 100%.

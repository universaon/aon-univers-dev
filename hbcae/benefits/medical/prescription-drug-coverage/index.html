Prescription Drug Coverage


You will automatically be enrolled in prescription drug coverage through CVS Caremark when you elect medical coverage. Through CVS’s nationwide pharmacy network, you will receive discounted prices, and you can fill your 90-day maintenance prescriptions right at the pharmacy and pay the lower mail-order copay.


CVS CAREMARK PROGRAMS FOR YOU

CVS Caremark administers a number of important programs to improve the quality and affordability of your prescription drug coverage. Below are some programs to be aware of:


FORMULARY MANAGEMENT

In addition to the programs below, CVS Caremark updates its Performance Drug List on a quarterly basis to ensure that members have access to the most effective and affordable medications. There are often multiple drugs available to treat the same condition. Based on their review of the latest information on drugs, products and prices, medications are continually added or removed from the list.

Products may be added to the drug list because they have demonstrated improved efficiency and/or a more convenient dosage option. Products may be removed due to the requirement of less convenient dosage therapy, more side effects or an increase in pricing compared to other available options. CVS Caremark’s drug list is updated regularly to include the products that help you get the most from your prescription benefits.

To view the most current version of the drug list, visit the Caremark website at www.caremark.com.


Dispense as Written

Dispense as Written (DAW) can be noted on a prescription. It means the pharmacist should not make any substitutions and must dispense only that drug when filling the prescription. This is an indicator from a physician that, even if a generic alternative is available, it cannot be used. Note that you will still be charged the generic copay as well as the difference in cost between the generic and brand drugs even if DAW is indicated on your script.

Drug Savings Review

This program identifies drug safety concerns and opportunities for more cost-effective therapy to keep you safe and maximize your savings.


Mail-Order Program for Maintenance Medications

Associates can receive 90-day supplies of maintenance medications mailed directly to their home or to their local CVS pharmacy at lower mail-order rates.


Specialty Guideline Management

This program supports the appropriate use of specialty drugs and helps ensure that Associates experience expected outcomes while on drug therapy.


Specialty Preferred Drugs Strategy

A preferred drug strategy takes the Specialty Guideline Management review process one step further to make sure Associates try preferred therapy drugs before non-preferred medications.


Step Therapy

Generic alternatives contain the same active ingredients as their brand-name counterparts and are generally available at a much lower cost. The Step Therapy program requires the use of cost-effective generic alternatives within the same therapeutic class before certain brand-name medications can be dispensed.

Individual Maximums:

The plan deductibles and out-of-pocket maximums are based on the coverage tier you elected. If you are enrolled in Associate Only coverage, you would be responsible to meet the “single” deductible and out-of-pocket maximum. If you are enrolled in Associate + Spouse/Domestic Partner, Associate + Children or Associate + Family coverage, the “family” deductible and out-of-pocket maximum apply. In addition, there are individual maximums for the Silver and Bronze plans. This means if you elect family coverage (Associate + Spouse/Domestic Partner, Associate + Children or Associate + Family), the most an individual family member would be responsible to pay out-of-pocket for the year when using in-network providers would be $6,550.


Using Generic Drugs Can Help Save You Money

Generics are FDA-approved drugs that contain the same active ingredients as their brand-name equivalents; they are generally identical in dosage and strength. However, generic drugs are typically sold at discounted prices compared to the brand-name version.

Under HBC’s prescription drug coverage, when a generic drug is available but your prescription calls for a brand-name drug, you will pay the difference in cost between the brand name and the generic, plus the generic copayment.

If you think a generic form of your prescription drug may be a better option for you, talk with your doctor.


FILLING YOUR PRESCRIPTIONS

When it comes time to fill your prescription, you have a few choices to consider:


Retail:

At local network pharmacies, including major chains like CVS, Kmart, Rite Aid and Walgreens, you can fill three 30-day supplies of a maintenance medication before moving to a 90-day supply through mail order or Mail Order at Retail. Some local pharmacies are also included in the network, so be sure to check before you fill your prescription. Any non-maintenance medications such as antibiotics can be filled at any in network retail pharmacy.


Mail Order and Mail Order at Retail

You must fill your maintenance medication prescriptions through the CVS Caremark mail-order program or at a CVS drugstore to receive benefits from the plan.

To fill a mail-order prescription, download the mail-order form from www.caremark.com and send the completed form to CVS Caremark, along with your original 90-day prescription and payment. You can also refill your prescription online at www.caremark.com ,by calling 1-888-202-1652 or through the mail.

Similarly, you can fill your prescription through Mail Order at Retail, which allows you to order your 90-day maintenance medication at your local CVS pharmacy and pay the discounted mail-order price. With this option, you get the cost savings of the mail-order program and the flexibility to pick up your prescription at a local CVS pharmacy.

Whichever option you choose, be sure to follow the suggested refill dates on the CVS Caremark label of your prescriptions, and remember to renew them three weeks before your current prescription is due to run out.
